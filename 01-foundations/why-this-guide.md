# Why I Wrote This

I'll be honest: I was skeptical when Copilot first launched. Another productivity tool promising to change everything? I'd seen that movie before. But I started using it anyway, and within six months I had to admit I was wrong. Not about everything, the hype was and still is overblown, but wrong about the fundamental utility. These tools are genuinely useful, just not in the ways most people think.

That said, the hype has gotten out of control. Vendors claim 55% productivity gains. LinkedIn influencers post about 10x developer output. Meanwhile, the most rigorous study we have, from METR in July 2025, found developers were actually 19% SLOWER with AI tools. What's going on?

The truth, as usual, is complicated. After two years of daily use across multiple projects, countless experiments, and way too many hours reading research papers, I've developed opinions about where these tools help, where they hurt, and how to actually get value from them.

Fair warning: I'm going to be opinionated. I'll tell you which tools I think are worth paying for and which ones aren't. I'll share stories from my own experience, some embarrassing. And I'll call out vendor BS when I see it. If you want a balanced, everything-is-wonderful overview, plenty of those exist. This isn't one of them.
